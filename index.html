🐾 FIELD K9 EMERGENCY MEDICAL NOMENCLATURE
1. Canine CPR Protocol
Place the canine asset on a firm surface, right side down (left side facing upward). Locate the cardiac field area directly where the left elbow meets the chest wall frame. Compress chest wall depth by 1/3 to 1/2 thickness at a sustained rhythm of 100 to 120 beats per minute. Coordinate chest wall work with breathing cycles: administer 30 compression strikes followed cleanly by 2 quick rescue breaths sealed directly into the canine's snout vectors.
2. Venomous Snake Bite Countermeasures
Maintain absolute asset immobilization loop to slow lymphatic circulatory speed. Do not cut the puncture marks or use mechanical suction accessories. Wash the site lane with clean water lines. Remove collar gear immediately to prevent respiratory throat closure if the bite vector is located on the neck or jaw. Clear team lanes and route directly to anti-venom distribution hubs.
3. Penetrating Gunshot Wound Trauma
Locate wound entry and exit tracts immediately. If an entry breach is in the thoracic chest wall cavity (sucking chest wound), seal the tract immediately with an occlusive chest wrap or clean plastic material to maintain necessary lung box pressure. Apply heavy localized hand compression with gauze lines to staunch bleeding. Do not apply tourniquet straps to body wall structures.
4. Critical Heat Exhaustion Matrix
Evacuate handler team and canine asset to shade or temperature-controlled areas instantly. Saturate abdomen, groin channels, and paw pads with cool, continuous water flow lanes. Do not pack the canine asset in direct freezing ice layers, as this triggers rapid vasoconstriction. Offer small, controlled amounts of water if the canine is fully conscious. Monitor internal vitals constantly.
5. Excessive Arterial Bleeding Control
Apply persistent manual hand pressure straight onto the arterial source wound using heavy sterile gauze sheets. Pack deep tissue lac tracts firmly with hemostatic gauze. Wrap site tightly with elastic pressure bands. If severe arterial bleeding breaches extremity structures, prepare a tactical emergency tourniquet band positioned explicitly above the wound point.
6. Paw and Eye Injuries
Paw Lacerations: Flush the entry pads with sterile saline. Apply a non-adherent dressing pack, wrap securely with protective tactical tape layers, and keep moisture-free to avoid pad separation.
Ocular Emergencies: Prevent the canine asset from rubbing the site. Flush the eye socket continuously with sterile eyewash drops to clear foreign particulate. Do not apply localized pressure wraps over the eye globe frame.
